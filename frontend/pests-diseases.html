Pest and Disease Library

Identify common threats to your crops and learn how to effectively manage them for a healthy harvest.

General Pests

These are common insect pests that can cause significant damage to your plants by feeding on leaves, stems, or roots.

1. Aphids (Piercing-Sucking Insects)

Description:

Small, soft-bodied insects (green, black, or red) often found in clusters on new growth, leaf undersides, and stems. They can have wings or be wingless.

Symptoms:

  • Stunted, yellow, or distorted new leaves.
  • Sticky residue on leaves (honeydew).
  • Presence of sooty mold (a black fungus that grows on honeydew).

Damage:

They suck sap from the plant, weakening it. They can also transmit viral diseases.

Solution:

  • Manual: Spray with a strong jet of water to dislodge them.
  • Biological: Introduce natural predators like ladybugs or lacewings.
  • Organic: Apply a horticultural oil or neem oil spray.
  • Chemical: Use an appropriate insecticide if the infestation is severe.

2. Spider Mites (Mites)

Description:

Tiny, spider-like pests that are hard to see with the naked eye. They thrive in hot, dry conditions.

Symptoms:

  • Tiny yellow or white specks on leaves.
  • Fine webbing on leaves and stems, especially on the undersides.
  • Bronze or yellow discoloration of leaves.

Damage:

They puncture plant cells and suck out the contents, causing a "stippling" effect on the leaves.

Solution:

  • Manual: Rinse the plant thoroughly with water, focusing on the undersides of leaves.
  • Organic: Apply neem oil or insecticidal soap.
  • Environmental: Increase humidity around the plant.

3. Caterpillars (Chewing Insects)

Description:

Larval stage of moths and butterflies. Their appearance varies widely.

Symptoms:

  • Ragged holes in leaves.
  • Missing leaves or defoliation.
  • Chewed stems or fruits.
  • Visible droppings (frass).

Damage:

They chew through plant tissue, causing significant and rapid damage.

Solution:

  • Manual: Hand-pick and remove them.
  • Biological: Use a biological insecticide containing Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt), which is toxic only to caterpillars.
  • Organic: Apply a deterrent spray like one made with garlic or chili pepper.

4. Whiteflies (Piercing-Sucking Insects)

Description:

Tiny, moth-like insects that are often found on the undersides of leaves. They fly up in a white cloud when disturbed.

Symptoms:

  • Yellowing and wilting leaves.
  • Sticky honeydew and sooty mold.
  • Stunted growth.

Damage:

Similar to aphids, they weaken the plant by sucking out sap and can transmit viruses.

Solution:

  • Manual: Use a hand-held vacuum to remove them, or spray with water.
  • Organic: Apply insecticidal soap or neem oil.
  • Prevention: Use yellow sticky traps to catch adults.

General Diseases

These are common plant diseases, often caused by fungi or bacteria, which can affect plant health and productivity.

1. Powdery Mildew (Fungal)

Symptoms:

  • A white, powdery, flour-like substance on the leaves, stems, and flowers.
  • Leaves may become yellowed, distorted, and stunted.

Cause:

A fungal disease that thrives in warm, dry climates with high humidity at night.

Solution:

  • Cultural: Improve air circulation around plants. Avoid overhead watering.
  • Organic: Apply a mixture of baking soda and water or a sulfur-based fungicide.
  • Chemical: Use a fungicide specifically for powdery mildew.

2. Downy Mildew (Fungal)

Symptoms:

  • Yellow, light green, or white spots on the upper surface of leaves.
  • A fuzzy, white-to-gray mold or "downy" growth on the underside of the leaves.
  • Infected leaves turn brown and drop off.

Cause:

A fungal-like organism that flourishes in cool, wet conditions.

Solution:

  • Cultural: Avoid overhead watering. Ensure good drainage.
  • Chemical: Apply a copper-based fungicide.

3. Blight (Fungal/Bacterial)

Symptoms:

  • Rapid browning, wilting, and death of large sections of the plant (leaves, stems, flowers).
  • Dark, water-soaked spots on leaves and stems that expand quickly.

Cause:

Can be caused by various fungi or bacteria.

Solution:

  • Cultural: Remove and destroy infected plant parts immediately.
  • Chemical: Apply a fungicide or bactericide depending on the cause.
  • Prevention: Use resistant plant varieties and ensure proper spacing.

4. Root Rot (Fungal)

Symptoms:

  • Wilting and yellowing of leaves, even when the soil is moist.
  • Stunted growth.
  • Roots are brown, soft, and mushy instead of firm and white.

Cause:

Fungi in the soil that attack roots, usually in poorly drained, waterlogged conditions.

Solution:

  • Cultural: Improve soil drainage. Repot plants with fresh, well-draining soil. Reduce watering.
  • Prevention: Avoid overwatering.

5. Viral Diseases (e.g., Mosaic Virus)

Symptoms:

  • Mottled, yellow and green patches on leaves (mosaic pattern).
  • Distorted, curled, or crinkled leaves.
  • Stunted or abnormal growth.

Cause:

Viruses spread by insects (especially aphids and whiteflies) or contaminated tools.

Solution:

  • There is no cure for viral diseases.
  • Prevention is key: Control insect vectors and sterilize tools.
  • Action: Remove and destroy infected plants immediately to prevent the spread to others.
